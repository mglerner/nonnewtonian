For students

Your job is to find a scientist who made a real contribution to physics but isn't one of the handful of famous names at the front of the book, then work out where in your course their work fits.

How to write a good entry

  • Who are they? Name, when and where they worked, a sentence of context.
  • What did they do? The contribution, and why it mattered to physics.
  • Where does it fit? A chapter of your textbook, plus a sentence on why.
  • Cite your sources. Wikipedia is fine. Quote if you like, but don't plagiarize.
  • Add a photo if you can, ideally from a source that allows reuse (Wikimedia Commons, the IF/THEN Collection).

Try to pick someone nobody else in your class is writing about. Check the collection and the wanted list first.
